Exceptions [EXCEPT] 

Rules related to using exceptions.
RULES

Do not abuse exceptions as flow control statements [EXCEPT.AEFC]
Do not use instanceof in a catch block to check the exception type [EXCEPT.AIOC]
Catch all "Throwable" objects which may be thrown in the body of certain methods [EXCEPT.CATO]
Avoid catch clauses with the same content [EXCEPT.CDUPL]
Avoid using finally block for closing resource only [EXCEPT.CLFIN]
Always chain thrown exceptions [EXCEPT.CTE]
Do not throw exceptions from constructors of "public" non-"final" classes [EXCEPT.EPNFC]
Do not hide 'catch' blocks [EXCEPT.HCB]
Declare all fields of user-defined 'Exception' as "final" [EXCEPT.IMMEX]
Do not declare any thrown exceptions in the 'main()' method [EXCEPT.MTE]
Do not catch the 'java.lang.Error' object [EXCEPT.NCERR]
Do not catch 'NullPointerException' [EXCEPT.NCNPE]
Ensure that the 'parse' methods of the numeric classes do not throw unhandled "NumberFormatExceptions" [EXCEPT.NFE]
Do not throw exception types which are too general or are unchecked exceptions [EXCEPT.NTERR]
Do not throw 'NullPointerException' [EXCEPT.NTNPE]
Rethrow certain exceptions if they are caught [EXCEPT.RTERR]
Do not throw exceptions from the constructors of exception classes [EXCEPT.TEFEC]
Place "try/catch/finally" blocks outside of loops [EXCEPT.TRY]
Exception messages must meet minimum character requirement [EXCEPT.TSCE]
